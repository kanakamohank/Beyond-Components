\vspace{10pt}
\noindent\small\textbf{Reading guide.}
Each cell lists the 4 heads (layer$\cdot$head) the routed ensemble uses for that
(model, domain) pair, in rank order. The bottom row per model is the scalar
$\alpha$ the per-domain calibrator selected to hit the target SNR
(Gemma alphas are large because its residual-stream norms are substantially
bigger than the other models'). Heads that recur across domains (e.g.\
GPT-2 L9~H7; Phi-3 L24~H14; Llama L22~H12, L22~H14) are the \emph{multi-domain}
heads; heads that appear in a single column are \emph{domain-specific}. This is
the source of the routing analysis in \texttt{head\_domain\_routing.md}:
GPT-2 and Gemma are \emph{specialized} (spec index $\geq 0.80$), Phi-3 and
Llama are \emph{mixed} (spec index $\approx 0.50$, which is why both per-domain
routing and per-domain $\alpha$ help those two).

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent\small\textbf{Note on Gemma religion.} None of the four top heads
Gemma routes for religion pass the SNR gate --- the fallback is retained with
a high $\alpha$. This is consistent with Gemma's specialization profile
(single dominant gender head at L21~H4) and explains the weaker StereoSet
religion effect observed for Gemma.
