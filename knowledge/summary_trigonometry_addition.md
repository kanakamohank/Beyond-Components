## TL;DR
First representation-level explanation of LLM mathematical capability, discovering that numbers are represented as a "generalized helix" and arithmetic operations work through trigonometric helix manipulation - the "Clock" algorithm.

## Problem
Understanding how language models perform mathematical computations at the representation level, moving beyond behavioral analysis to mechanistic understanding of numerical reasoning in neural networks.

## Method
**Helix Discovery Framework:**
1. **Representation Analysis** - Model influential MLP outputs, attention head outputs, and neuron preactivations
2. **Geometric Characterization** - Identify helical structure in number representations
3. **Clock Algorithm** - Reverse-engineer arithmetic through helix manipulation
4. **Causal Intervention** - Verify understanding through targeted interventions

**Technical Approach:**
- Focus on mid-sized LLMs mathematical capabilities
- Analyze geometric structure of numerical representations
- Test causal necessity of helical representations

## Key Results
- **Generalized Helix**: Numbers represented as points on a helical structure in representation space
- **Clock Algorithm**: Arithmetic operations performed through systematic helix manipulation
- **Broad Applicability**: Helix causally implicated for addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and modular arithmetic
- **Mechanistic Completeness**: First complete representation-level explanation of mathematical capability
- **Trigonometric Basis**: Mathematical operations fundamentally based on trigonometric transformations

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Limited to mid-sized LLMs - unclear scaling to larger models
- Geometric interpretation may not generalize to all mathematical reasoning
- Computational overhead of helical analysis
- Relationship to other mathematical reasoning mechanisms unclear
