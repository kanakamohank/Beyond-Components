\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Mechanistic interpretability aims to reverse-engineer the internal
computations of neural language models. Two threads dominate: circuit
analysis, which localizes behavior to \emph{components}---specific
attention heads or MLPs
\citep{elhage2021mathematical,wang2023interpretability,conmy2023acdc,meng2022rome}---and
linear feature discovery, which searches for low-dimensional causal
\emph{directions} in activation space
\citep{park2024linearrep,geiger2024das,marks2024geometry,engels2024not,cunningham2023sparse,templeton2024scaling}.
A central question sits between them: what is the atomic functional
unit? Whole heads are coarse; arbitrary residual-stream directions are
not tied to any specific weight matrix. We argue the right unit is
finer than a head but attached to the head's own weights: the singular
directions---and, more tightly, singular \emph{pairs}---of its
output-value map $W_{OV} = W_V W_O$.

This paper develops that claim in two stages.

\textbf{Stage 1 (sub-component units).}
We first establish that individual OV singular directions are causal
control axes. On GPT-2 Small and the Gender Pronoun (GP) task, a
learnable-mask circuit discovery procedure applied to the SVD of each
head's OV and QK matrices isolates a sparse set of five
gender-relevant directions. Continuous scalar steering along these
directions produces monotonic dose-response curves: GP accuracy drops
from $84.00\%$ baseline to $32.67\%$ under a single-sweep intervention
and to $0.00\%$ (a full $100\%$ he$\to$she flip) under $\sigma\times 2.0$
amplification. The control is \emph{asymmetric}: masculine-coded
directions steer the model more strongly than feminine-coded ones, and
per-direction calibration of the feminine-only subset narrows but does
not close this gap.

\textbf{Stage 2 (rank-$2$ geometry).}
The directions recovered in Stage~1 are not isolated $1$D axes. For
head $h$ at layer $\ell$ with SVD
$W_{OV}^{(\ell,h)} = U\Sigma V^{\top}$, a \emph{semantic compass} is a
pair of columns $(u_i, u_j)$ such that (i) vocabulary decoding of
$u_i$ and $u_j$ through $W_U$ yields a coherent angular ordering of
category tokens, and (ii) residual-stream injection
$\alpha\sigma(\cos\theta\,u_i + \sin\theta\,u_j)$ at the head's own
layer produces a logit-difference response
$\mathrm{LD}(\theta) \approx \mu + A\cos(\theta - \phi)$ whose
amplitude scales linearly with $\alpha$ and whose phase is stable
across $\alpha$. The GP head GPT-2 L9H7 (direction SV$_1$ in Stage~1)
and the GP head GPT-2 L10H9 (direction SV$_0$ in Stage~1) are both
rediscovered as the azimuthal axes of genuine $2$D compasses,
$(u_1, u_2)$ and $(u_0, u_3)$, satisfying the joint amplitude-linearity
+ phase-stability test. The same construction replicates in Phi-3
Mini (L24H10, L28H1), Gemma-2-$2$B (L21H4), and Llama-$3.2$-$3$B
(L26H14), and surfaces two \emph{non-pronoun} compasses in Phi-3: a
temporal-unit dial (month vs.\ hour, L24H17) and an entity-type dial
(item vs.\ operation, L24H28). A blind causal scan over
$864$--$4032$ candidate $(L, H, u_i, u_j)$ planes per model, which
never consults vocabulary decoding, recovers the same heads with
head-level concentration significant at $p < 10^{-6}$ against a
full-OV null. Finally, rotating the compass toward its feminine pole
\emph{and} using it without retraining on occupation prompts
transfers the Stage-$1$ behavioral effect to the rank-$2$ setting,
and the asymmetric-control phenomenon persists.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sub-component causal units (\cref{sec:bc}).} We
    recover a sparse set of OV singular directions in GPT-2 Small
    that causally control the GP task, show that scalar steering
    produces dose-response curves, and demonstrate $100\%$ flip
    under $\sigma\!\times\!2.0$ amplification.
    \item \textbf{From $1$D directions to $2$D compasses
    (\cref{sec:method}).} We formalize the semantic compass as a
    pair of OV singular vectors and define the joint
    amplitude-linearity + phase-stability test that separates
    causal compasses from geometric accidents.
    \item \textbf{Four-model gender compass (\cref{sec:gender}).}
    Every compass produces $R^2 \ge 0.993$ amplitude linearity and
    $\Delta\phi \le 3^\circ$ phase stability across
    $\alpha \in \{1, 3, 10\}$ and across four models spanning two
    orders of magnitude in parameters.
    \item \textbf{Blind causal scan (\cref{sec:scan}).} A scan of
    all top-$4$ SV-pairs per head rediscovers the same sites
    without decoding, with head-level concentration significance
    $p < 10^{-6}$, and surfaces heads the decode survey missed.
    \item \textbf{Non-pronoun compasses in Phi-3
    (\cref{sec:other}).} The recipe generalizes off the gender
    axis: a temporal-unit dial and an entity-type dial in Phi-3,
    both with linear $\alpha$-scaling and stable phase.
    \item \textbf{Rank-$2$ downstream transfer and asymmetry
    (\cref{sec:downstream}).} The Stage-$1$ GP-to-occupation
    transfer and the Stage-$1$ masculine-vs-feminine asymmetry
    both persist under rank-$2$ compass intervention, confirming
    that the compass is not only a diagnostic but a steering
    primitive with the same behavioral signatures as its $1$D
    projection.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Circuit discovery.} Circuit analysis reverse-engineers
behavior into subsets of heads and MLPs
\citep{olah2020zoom,elhage2021mathematical,wang2023interpretability,conmy2023acdc,nanda2023progress,meng2022rome,marks2024sparse}.
Head-level ablation and patching are the standard tools. We work
one level finer: inside a head, where the OV map's singular
structure is the interface between the head's ``read-in'' and
``write-out'' behavior.

\paragraph{Linear feature discovery.} The linear representation
hypothesis \citep{park2024linearrep}, DAS
\citep{geiger2024das}, geometry-of-truth probes
\citep{marks2024geometry}, sparse autoencoders
\citep{cunningham2023sparse,templeton2024scaling}, and function
vectors \citep{todd2024function} all search for low-dimensional
causal features. These methods search in \emph{activation space};
our compasses are read directly from the \emph{weight matrix}, are
localized to a specific attention head, and give a continuous
family of $\theta$-rotations rather than a single direction.
Helical / circular numerical features \citep{engels2024not} are
the closest geometric precedent, though they are estimated via
probes on cached activations rather than read off the OV weights.

\paragraph{Activation steering.} Activation addition
\citep{turner2023actadd} and inference-time intervention
\citep{li2023inference} add a fixed vector to the residual stream
to steer generation. Compass-based steering replaces the fixed
vector with a continuous rank-$2$ dial: each rotation angle in
$[0, 2\pi)$ is a valid intervention, and the amplitude-phase
decomposition gives a principled way to pick one.

\section{Sub-Component Units: Directions as Causal Axes}
\label{sec:bc}

This section establishes the Stage-$1$ claim: individual OV
singular directions causally control GP behavior in GPT-2 Small.

\subsection{Circuit discovery by learnable masking}
\label{sec:bc_mask}

For each attention head at layer $\ell$ we compute two SVDs: the
effective OV map
$W_{OV}^{(\ell,h)} = W_O^{(\ell,h)} W_V^{(\ell,h)}
  = U^{\text{OV}}\,\mathrm{diag}(S^{\text{OV}})\,(V^{\text{OV}})^{\top}$,
which controls residual-stream writes, and the effective QK map
$W_{QK}^{(\ell,h)}
  = U^{\text{QK}}\,\mathrm{diag}(S^{\text{QK}})\,(V^{\text{QK}})^{\top}$,
which controls attention patterns. We attach a learnable soft mask
$m_i \in [0, 1]$ to every singular value, substitute
$S \gets m \odot S$ during the forward pass, and train the mask
with the composite loss
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \mathrm{KL}\!\left(P_{\text{full}} \,\|\, P_{\text{masked}}\right)
  + \lambda_1 \,\|m\|_1,
\end{equation}
where $P_{\text{full}}$ is the output distribution of the unmasked
model and $P_{\text{masked}}$ uses the gated SVD. Training with
KL target $0.10$, L1 target $3500$, learning rate $0.02$, and
early stopping (patience $5$) converges to a sparse configuration
in which the OV masks identify a handful of gender-relevant
directions (\cref{fig:mask_progression}).

\subsection{Scalar causal steering}
\label{sec:bc_steering}

Given a discovered direction $(u_i, \sigma_i, v_i)$ at layer
$\ell$, we read off its per-token scalar coefficient
$\alpha_i^{(t)} = V'^{(t)} \cdot u_i$, where $V'^{(t)}$ is the
pre-attention residual at token $t$. A scalar intervention
$\alpha_i^{(t)} \to \alpha_i'^{(t)}$ perturbs the residual stream
by $\Delta_{\text{resid}} =
  (\alpha_i'^{(t)} - \alpha_i^{(t)})\,\sigma_i\,v_i$.
We evaluate four steering modes: \emph{mean swap} (replace with
opposite-gender empirical mean), \emph{additive}
($\alpha_i' = \alpha_i + \delta$), \emph{sigma-scaled}
($\alpha_i' = \alpha_i + k\sigma_{\text{emp}}$), and a continuous
\emph{sweep} over $k$.

\subsection{Behavioral effect on the GP task}
\label{sec:bc_gp}

\cref{fig:bc_sweep} summarizes the effect on the GP task
($150$ he prompts, $156$ she prompts). On HE prompts, sweeping
toward feminine values reduces accuracy from
$84.00\% \to 32.67\%$ and target logit-difference from
$2.525 \to 0.228$. On SHE prompts, sweeping toward masculine
values is markedly weaker: accuracy drops only to $53.21\%$
($2.840 \to 1.480$ LD). Under $\sigma\!\times\!2.0$ smart-ablation
amplification on HE prompts, accuracy collapses to $0.00\%$ with
target LD $-2.143$, i.e.\ a full $100\%$ he$\to$she flip.
Direction-selective interventions confirm specificity: masculine-only
swap on HE reduces accuracy to $45.33\%$; feminine-only swap on SHE
reduces it to $57.05\%$.

The asymmetry---strong masculine control, weaker feminine
control---is the single most consistent phenomenon across every
Stage-$1$ experiment. We return to it in
\cref{sec:downstream}.

\paragraph{Summary.} Five OV singular directions in GPT-2 Small
causally control GP behavior; scalar steering along them is
dose-responsive and can be amplified to full behavioral reversal.
The rest of the paper asks what \emph{geometry} these directions
live in.

\section{From Directions to Compasses}
\label{sec:method}

The Stage-$1$ directions include GPT-2 L9H7.SV$_1$ (masculine) and
GPT-2 L10H9.SV$_0$ (feminine). Are these isolated $1$D axes, or
are they one column of a richer $2$D structure? We now define the
rank-$2$ test that decides this.

\subsection{Compass plane}
\label{sec:plane}

For attention head $h$ at layer $\ell$ we compute the combined OV
map
$W_{OV}^{(\ell,h)} = W_V^{(\ell,h)}\,W_O^{(\ell,h)} \in
  \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{model}}}$
and its SVD $U\,\Sigma\,V^{\top}$ with columns
$u_1, \ldots, u_{d_{\text{model}}}$ and singular values
$\sigma_1 \ge \sigma_2 \ge \cdots \ge 0$. A \emph{compass plane}
for the head is an ordered pair of columns
$\mathcal{C}_{i,j}^{(\ell, h)} = \mathrm{span}(u_i, u_j)$, $i \ne j$.
We always take left-singular columns, the \emph{write-out}
directions, because they correspond to what the head writes into
the residual stream rather than what it reads.

\subsection{Causal $\alpha$-sweep}
\label{sec:alpha_sweep}

The compass test is a causal injection that varies an \emph{angle}
$\theta$ and a \emph{scale} $\alpha$:
\begin{equation}
v(\theta, \alpha) = \alpha\,\sigma_i\,\cos\theta\,u_i
  + \alpha\,\sigma_j\,\sin\theta\,u_j.
\label{eq:alpha_sweep}
\end{equation}
For a prompt $p$ whose final-token residual stream just before
layer $\ell$ is $r$, we hook at
\texttt{blocks.\{$\ell$\}.hook\_resid\_pre} and replace
$r \gets r + v(\theta, \alpha)$; the remainder of the forward pass
runs unmodified. We record
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{LD}(\theta, \alpha; p) =
  \mathrm{logit}_{t_{+}} - \mathrm{logit}_{t_{-}}
\end{equation}
for antipodal probe tokens $(t_{+}, t_{-})$, average over three
prompts per condition, sweep $\theta$ through $36$ angles (step
$10^\circ$), and run $\alpha \in \{1, 3, 10\}$.

For each curve we fit
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{LD}(\theta) \approx \mu + A\,\cos(\theta - \phi)
\label{eq:sinusoid}
\end{equation}
by the first DFT bin and record mean $\mu$, amplitude $A$, phase
$\phi$, and the angles of maximum / minimum response.

\paragraph{The joint test.} A random plane produces neither
amplitude nor phase structure. A plane that correlates with the
category without being causally read produces some amplitude but
no dependence on $\alpha$. A truly causal compass satisfies two
conditions simultaneously: \emph{amplitude linearity}
($A(\alpha)$ grows linearly with $\alpha$, $R^2 \ge 0.95$) and
\emph{phase stability} ($\phi$ drift $\Delta\phi \le 10^\circ$
across $\alpha \in \{1, 3, 10\}$). We enforce both.

\subsection{Nine-test battery}
\label{sec:battery}

We complement \cref{eq:alpha_sweep} with a battery of eight null
and falsification tests: vocabulary decoding, $2\pi$-cyclicity,
label permutation, random-plane null, principal-angles check
against unrelated heads, scan-planes (target pair is
$\arg\max$ over $i,j \le 10$), semantic ablation (zeroing
$P_{\mathcal{C}} r$ collapses category-conditioned logits),
causal patching (swapping $P_{\mathcal{C}} r$ between
$\pm$-context prompts flips the logit sign), and an end-to-end
self-test. Full specifications are in \cref{app:battery}.

\subsection{Models}

We use TransformerLens \citep{nanda2022transformerlens} with
\texttt{HookedTransformer} on bfloat16. Target heads:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{GPT-2 Small}
      \citep{radford2019language}: L9H7 $(u_1, u_2)$
      (decode-discovered) and L10H9 $(u_0, u_3)$
      (scan-discovered). Stage-$1$ directions L9H7.SV$_1$ and
      L10H9.SV$_0$ are individual columns of these planes.
    \item \textbf{Phi-3 Mini} \citep{abdin2024phi3}: L24H10
      $(u_0, u_1)$; L28H1 $(u_0, u_3)$; L24H17 $(u_1, u_2)$
      (temporal); L24H28 $(u_3, u_7)$ (entity).
    \item \textbf{Gemma-2-2B} \citep{team2024gemma}: L21H4
      $(u_0, u_2)$.
    \item \textbf{Llama-3.2-3B} \citep{grattafiori2024llama3}:
      L26H14 $(u_2, u_3)$ (scan-discovered).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Bridge to Stage-$1$.} The GPT-2 L9H7 row uses
$(u_1, u_2)$; Stage-$1$ isolated $u_1$ alone. The GPT-2 L10H9 row
uses $(u_0, u_3)$; Stage-$1$ isolated $u_0$ alone. In both cases
the Stage-$1$ $1$D direction is one column of the Stage-$2$ $2$D
compass plane, and the compass exposes additional structure: a
continuous $\theta$-family of interventions, of which the
Stage-$1$ direction is a single point.

\paragraph{Decoding the compass basis.} Projecting write
directions through $W_U$ reveals the vocabulary each pole of the
plane maps to. At the context-favoring pole the compass basis
decodes coherently: \emph{his, He, he} vs.\ \emph{herself, her,
She} on GPT-2 L9H7; \emph{he, him, his, ihn} plus Japanese and
Chinese masculine pronouns vs.\ \emph{she, her} plus Japanese,
Chinese, and Korean feminine pronouns on Gemma-2-2B (the compass
is cross-lingual); matching patterns on Phi-3 and Llama. Multiple
languages' pronouns co-localize in the same plane, consistent
with a shared gender axis in the write-out subspace.

\section{Blind Causal Scan}
\label{sec:scan}

The compasses above were surfaced by decoding the top-$k$ singular
vectors through $W_U$ and reading promoted vocabulary. That recipe
is biased toward visually clean cases. To rule out selection bias,
we complement decode-driven discovery with a blind causal scan
that applies the two-pillar test of \cref{tab:gender} to every
$(L, H, u_i, u_j)$ candidate without inspecting decode.

\paragraph{Setup.} For each model we enumerate all
$(\text{layer} \times \text{head} \times \binom{4}{2})$ planes from
the top-$4$ SVs of each head's $W_{OV}$: $864$ planes for GPT-2,
$1344$ for Phi-3, $240$ for Gemma, $4032$ for Llama. We run the
gender $\alpha$-sweep in-context with $\alpha \in \{3, 10\}$ and
$12$ angles, fit a sinusoid, and pass a plane if $R^2 \ge 0.95$,
$\Delta\phi \le 10^\circ$, and slope $A(10)/10 \ge 0.2$. Two null
baselines: a \emph{top-$4$} null (random orthonormal pair from the
head's top-$4$ write subspace) and a \emph{full-OV} null (random
orthonormal pair from $\mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}}}$).

Every model exceeds its full-OV null by a non-overlapping margin.
Against the top-$4$ null the rates overlap, showing that the
top-$4$ write subspace is itself semantically enriched; the
discriminating statistic is \emph{head-level concentration}, not
aggregate rate. On GPT-2, $15/16$ passing planes come from three
heads (L9H7, L10H9, L11H1); under the independence null implied
by aggregate rate, the probability of a single head hosting
$\ge 4$ passes is $\binom{6}{4}(0.02)^4 \approx 10^{-6}$. On
Llama, $7/9$ passes concentrate at two heads (L26H14 with $6/6$
passes, L20H13 with $3/6$). Phi-3 concentrates at L24 and L28.

\paragraph{Strengthened Monte-Carlo nulls.} On Phi-3 L28H1 we run
a $1000$-trial random-plane null (observed $= 40.77$, random-mean
$= 20.47$, $p = 0.142$; long-tailed because random directions
occasionally land on other semantic axes) and a $2000$-trial
label-permutation null (perm-mean $= 9.42$, perm-max $= 24.35$,
$p = 0.000$). Permutation is the sharp test.

\paragraph{The scan found heads the decode survey missed.} GPT-2
L10H9, Phi-3 L28H1, and Llama-3.2-3B L26H14 were not in the
original decode-based pick. L10H9's gender content is concentrated
on SV$_0$ alone (the decode browser preferred L9H7 because it
spreads content across two orthogonal SVs); the scan ranks L10H9
first by amplitude slope ($1.42$ vs.\ L9H7's $1.25$). Decode
browsing and causal scanning therefore do not pick up the same
heads; a complete survey should use both.

A two-harmonic fit separates \emph{two-way} antipodal dials from

$f(\theta) = \mu + A_1 \cos(\theta - \phi_1)

does.

\section{Rank-$2$ Downstream: Transfer and Asymmetry}
\label{sec:downstream}

A compass is useful only if a rank-$2$ rotation changes downstream
behavior without destroying fluency. We test this on the same
occupation-bias setup used in Stage-$1$ for scalar steering,
now with the full rank-$2$ compass acting as the steering
primitive. We use the GPT-2 L10H9 $(u_0, u_3)$ compass, rotated
toward its feminine pole (the $\theta$ at which the fitted
$\mathrm{LD}(\theta)$ is minimized on GP she-context prompts).

\subsection{GP $\to$ occupation transfer}
\label{sec:transfer}

We evaluate on $36$ occupation prompts of the form ``The
[occupation] said that'' spanning $12$ masculine-coded (doctor,
engineer, scientist, lawyer, CEO, architect, pilot, surgeon,
professor, programmer, executive, mechanic), $12$ feminine-coded
(nurse, receptionist, babysitter, housekeeper, assistant,
secretary, hairdresser, librarian, caretaker, maid, nanny,
florist), and $12$ neutral (teacher, writer, manager, student,
artist, journalist, accountant, therapist, consultant, designer,
analyst, photographer) professions. We measure the pronoun
bias-score change and the flip rate from stereotypical to
counter-stereotypical continuation. With the calibrated feminine
operating point ($\alpha = 2.00$, see \cref{sec:calib}), the
compass produces an overall transfer flip rate of $58.33\%$:
$83.33\%$ on masculine-coded occupations, $66.67\%$ on neutral
occupations, $25.00\%$ on feminine-coded occupations.

\subsection{Per-direction calibration of the feminine pole}
\label{sec:calib}

The Stage-$1$ asymmetry---masculine control stronger than
feminine---survives the promotion to rank-$2$. The raw
compass-at-$\alpha = 1$ setting passes only $19 / 150$ GP flips on
she-context prompts. We sweep the feminine-pole injection scale
$\alpha \in \{0.25, 0.50, \ldots, 2.30\}$ (\cref{fig:calib});
performance improves monotonically through
$\alpha = 1.25, 1.40, 1.55, 1.70$, breaking the $19 / 150$
plateau at $\alpha = 1.70$ ($20 / 150$ flips) and reaching
$21 / 150$ at $\alpha = 2.15$--$2.30$. Beyond $\alpha = 2.00$ the
transfer improves but GP accuracy on original she-context prompts
begins softening (from $36.67\%$ at $\alpha = 1.70$ to
$35.33\%$ at $\alpha = 2.30$). This defines two operating points:
a \emph{balanced} setting ($\alpha = 2.00$, strongest pre-bend
configuration) and an \emph{aggressive} setting ($\alpha = 2.30$,
strongest validated transfer at visible GP cost).

\paragraph{Asymmetry is a representation-level phenomenon.} Two
observations tie the calibration back to the main story.
(i) The asymmetry is already present in the
uncalibrated Stage-$1$ scalar-steering setup (\cref{sec:bc_gp})
and reproduces unchanged when the same head's compass plane is
swept. (ii) Calibration raises feminine-pole transfer (from the
$19 / 150$ plateau to $21 / 150$ at $\alpha = 2.15$) but cannot
bridge the gap to masculine-pole transfer. We interpret the
residual gap as a representational property of GPT-2 Small's
gender subspace rather than a calibration artifact: the feminine
pole is simply written at smaller magnitude by the OV map than the
masculine pole, and rotating inside the plane does not add
write-strength the head never possessed.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{What a compass is.} A semantic compass is a causal
feature localized to an attention head: a $2$D subspace of the
head's write-out space in which every angle is a valid
intervention. The Stage-$1$ finding that individual OV singular
directions are causal axes is therefore a $1$D projection of a
richer structure; the compass makes the orthogonal axis
explicit and turns a single-vector edit into a continuous
$\theta$-family.

\paragraph{Why OV-SVD works as a discovery device.} In a
transformer block, $W_{OV}$ determines the set of directions the
head can add to the residual stream. Its top SVD columns are
therefore the candidate ``output languages'' of the head. When a
pair of such columns corresponds to a categorical axis, it must
correspond to something the head \emph{actually writes}, not just
something we can correlate post hoc. This is the structural reason
OV-SVD is a stronger discovery device than probing residual-stream
activations directly.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
(i) \emph{Prompt scope.} Baselines and injections average over
three short ambiguous prompts per condition; robustness to
paraphrase is not yet quantified.
(ii) \emph{Axis breadth.} We demonstrate gender on four models
and temporal/entity on one (Phi-3). Sentiment, tense, formality,
and animacy remain untested.
(iii) \emph{Asymmetry resolution.} Per-direction calibration
narrows the masculine--feminine control gap but does not close it.
Whether the gap is a fundamental property of how gender is stored
in these models or can be mitigated by stronger calibration (or by
combining multiple compass planes) is open.

\paragraph{Conclusion.} Attention heads are not atomic: individual
OV singular directions are causal control axes, and pairs of those
directions form rank-$2$ compasses whose angular dial governs a
categorical distinction. The same weight-space recipe uncovers
gender dials in four decoder-only models and non-pronoun dials in
Phi-3, recovers the same heads in a blind causal scan, and
transfers the Stage-$1$ GP$\to$occupation bias effect (including
the masculine--feminine asymmetry) into a rank-$2$ steering
primitive. OV-SVD is a probe-free, architecture-agnostic tool for
discovering and editing low-dimensional causal features, with a
continuous family of interventions rather than a single vector.
