\begin{abstract}
We ask what the atomic functional unit of a transformer attention
head really is. Starting from the observation that individual OV
singular directions in GPT-2 Small causally control the
Gender-Pronoun (GP) task---a sparse set of five directions
recovered by learnable masking drives GP accuracy from $84\%$ to
$32.67\%$ under scalar steering, and to a full $100\%$ flip at
$\sigma\!\times\!2.0$---we show that these directions are not
isolated $1$D axes but live inside a coherent $2$D geometry. For an
attention head whose combined OV map has SVD
$W_{OV} = U\Sigma V^{\top}$, pairs of columns $(u_i, u_j)$ span
\emph{semantic compass planes} whose azimuthal angle codes a
categorical distinction and whose radius codes its magnitude. An
in-context rank-$2$ injection
$\alpha\sigma(\cos\theta\,u_i + \sin\theta\,u_j)$ produces a
sinusoidal logit-difference response whose amplitude grows linearly
in $\alpha$ ($R^2 \ge 0.993$) and whose phase is stable to
$\pm 3^\circ$ across $\alpha \in \{1,3,10\}$, in every one of four
pretrained decoder-only models---GPT-2 Small, Phi-3 Mini,
Gemma-2-2B, and Llama-3.2-3B. A blind causal scan over
$864$--$4032$ $(L,H,u_i,u_j)$ candidates per model recovers the
same heads without decoding, with head-level concentration
significant at $p < 10^{-6}$ against a full-OV null, and the scan
surfaces additional non-pronoun compasses in Phi-3 (month vs.\ hour,
item vs.\ operation). The downstream GP-to-occupation transfer and
the masculine-vs-feminine control asymmetry first observed at the
$1$D direction level both persist in the rank-$2$ setting and are
partially but not fully closed by per-direction calibration.
Together, this positions OV-SVD as an architecture-agnostic,
weight-space primitive: singular directions carry causal content,
and singular pairs carry a continuous $\theta$-family of
interventions.
\end{abstract}
