\begin{abstract}
We show that the SVD of a single attention head's $W_{OV}$ matrix
routinely exposes two-dimensional \emph{semantic compasses}: planes
whose azimuthal angle codes a categorical distinction (gender,
temporal unit, entity type) and whose radius codes its magnitude.
On four pretrained decoder-only models---GPT-2 Small, Phi-3 Mini,
Gemma-2-2B, and Llama-3.2-3B---an in-context rank-$2$ injection
$\alpha\,\sigma(\cos\theta\,u_i + \sin\theta\,u_j)$ produces a
sinusoidal $\mathrm{logit}(t_{+} - t_{-})$ response whose amplitude
grows linearly in $\alpha$ ($R^2 \ge 0.993$) and whose phase is
stable to $\pm 3^\circ$ across $\alpha \in \{1,3,10\}$. A blind
causal scan over all $(L, H, u_i, u_j)$ recovers the same heads
without consulting vocabulary, and head-level concentration is
significant at $p<10^{-6}$ against a full-OV null. Beyond
diagnosis, a single-head rank-$2$ edit monotonically decorrelates
WinoGender ($\mathrm{stereo\,corr}$ $0.638 \to 0.179$ on Phi-3 at
$\alpha=1$, $\Delta$PPL $+0.006$) and improves StereoSet ICAT by
$4\text{--}6$ points at matched language-model score. OV-SVD is
thus a weight-space, probe-free primitive for discovering and
editing low-dimensional causal features.
\end{abstract}
