\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Mechanistic interpretability splits between circuit analysis
\citep{elhage2021mathematical,wang2023interpretability,conmy2023acdc},
which identifies responsible components, and linear feature discovery
\citep{park2024linearrep,geiger2024das,marks2024geometry,engels2024not},
which searches for low-dimensional causal directions in the residual
stream. We connect the two at the level of a single attention head's
output-value map.

Let $W_{OV}^{(\ell,h)} = W_V^{(\ell,h)} W_O^{(\ell,h)}
\in \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{model}\times d_\text{model}}$, with SVD
$W_{OV}^{(\ell,h)} = U\Sigma V^{\top}$. A \emph{semantic compass}
is a pair $(u_i, u_j) \subset U$ such that (i) vocabulary decoding
$W_U^{\top}u_i$, $W_U^{\top}u_j$ gives a coherent angular ordering
of category tokens, and (ii) residual-stream injection
$\alpha\sigma(\cos\theta\,u_i + \sin\theta\,u_j)$ produces a
sinusoidal logit response whose phase is stable across $\alpha$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
(1)~We define the compass test (joint amplitude-linearity and
phase-stability) and validate it on four decoder-only models spanning
two orders of magnitude in parameters (\S\ref{sec:gender}).
(2)~A blind causal scan of all top-$4$ SV-pairs
recovers the same heads \emph{without} decoding, with head-level
concentration significance $p < 10^{-6}$ against a full-OV null
(\S\ref{sec:scan}).
(3)~A single-head rank-$2$ edit moves WinoGender stereotypical
coreference bias from $+0.60\text{--}0.73$ to near-zero with
perplexity drift under $0.08$ at the working scale, and improves
StereoSet gender ICAT by $4\text{--}6$ points on GPT-2 and Phi-3
(\S\ref{sec:downstream}).

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

For attention head $h$ at layer $\ell$, compute $W_{OV}$'s SVD and pick
a pair $(u_i, u_j)$ of left-singular vectors (\emph{write-out}
directions). A \emph{causal $\alpha$-sweep} injects
$v(\theta,\alpha) = \alpha\sigma_i\cos\theta\,u_i +
 \alpha\sigma_j\sin\theta\,u_j$
at \texttt{blocks.\{$\ell$\}.hook\_resid\_pre} during the forward pass,
records $\mathrm{LD}(\theta) = \mathrm{logit}(t_{+}) - \mathrm{logit}(t_{-})$
for antipodal probe tokens, and fits
$\mathrm{LD}(\theta)\approx\mu + A\cos(\theta - \phi)$. We report $A$
at $\alpha\in\{1,3,10\}$ and phase drift $\Delta\phi$. Compasses must
satisfy $R^2\ge 0.95$, $\Delta\phi\le 10^\circ$, and a linear
$A(\alpha)$ fit through the origin with $R^2 \ge 0.99$.

We use TransformerLens \citep{nanda2022transformerlens} on bfloat16.
The nine-test battery (decode-heads, cyclicity, permutation,
random-plane, principal-angles, scan-planes, semantic-ablate,
causal-patch, self-test) is described in
Appendix~\ref{app:battery}.

\paragraph{Decoding the basis.} At the masculine pole each head
promotes \emph{his, him, he} (plus cross-lingual equivalents:
German \emph{ihn}; Chinese \emph{ta-masc.}; French \emph{lui};
Polish \emph{jego}; Russian \emph{ego}); at the feminine pole
\emph{she, her, herself} and their Chinese, Japanese, Korean,
French counterparts. Multiple languages' pronouns co-localize in
the same plane, consistent with a shared gender axis in the
write-out subspace.

\paragraph{Storage vs.\ steering.} Plane ablation on the target
head removes $39.3\%$ of the gender signal on GPT-2 L10H9 and
$14.3\%$ on Gemma L21H4 (\emph{storage} compasses), but only
$0.6\%$ on GPT-2 L9H7 and $-0.6\%$ on Phi-3 L24H10
(\emph{steering} compasses). Both regimes respond
identically to causal $\alpha$-injection; the distinction is
head-level, not model-level. Phi-3 L28H1 (used for
Table~\ref{tab:gender}) is the scan-discovered storage-dominant
partner of steering head L24H10; principal angles between the
two fitted planes are $[86.4^\circ, 89.0^\circ]$ (orthogonal
planes, independent dials).

\section{Blind causal scan}
\label{sec:scan}

All compasses above were surfaced by decoding singular vectors, which
biases toward visually clean cases. We complement with a blind scan
over all $(L, H, u_i, u_j)$ from each head's top-$4$ SVs: $864$
planes for GPT-2, $1344$ for Phi-3, $240$ for Gemma, $4032$ for
Llama. Passing criterion: $R^2\ge 0.95$, $\Delta\phi\le 10^\circ$,
slope $A(10)/10\ge 0.2$.

Against the full-OV null every model exceeds by a non-overlapping
margin. The top-$4$ null overlaps with compass rate, showing that the
top-$4$ write subspace is itself semantically enriched. The
discriminating statistic is \emph{head-level concentration}: GPT-2
lands $15/16$ passing planes in three heads (L9H7, L10H9, L11H1), and
Llama lands $7/9$ in two heads (L26H14 with $6/6$ passes, L20H13 with
$3/6$). Under the independence null implied by aggregate rate, the
probability of any head hosting $\ge 4$ passes is
$\binom{6}{4}(0.02)^4 \approx 10^{-6}$. The scan also recovers heads
the decode-survey missed (GPT-2 L10H9, Phi-3 L28H1, Llama L26H14),
validating the two-method strategy.

\paragraph{Strengthened Monte-Carlo nulls.} On Phi-3 L28H1, a
$1000$-trial random-plane null gives observed $=40.77$,
random-mean $=20.47$, $p=0.142$ (long tail from random directions
landing on other semantic axes). A $2000$-trial label-permutation
null gives perm-mean $=9.42$, perm-max $=24.35$, $p=0.000$.
Permutation is the sharp test; random-plane becomes informative only
jointly with decode coherence and amplitude-linearity.

\section{Non-pronoun axes}
\label{sec:nonpronoun}

The same recipe surfaces two non-pronoun compasses in Phi-3:
a \emph{temporal unit} dial (L24H17, $u_1, u_2$,
$t_{\pm} = \emph{month}/\emph{hour}$) with
$A(1)/A(3)/A(10) = 0.69/2.10/7.60$ and phase drift $0.9^\circ$, and
an \emph{entity type} dial (L24H28, $u_3, u_7$,
$t_{\pm} = \emph{item}/\emph{operation}$) with
$A = 0.29/0.88/3.06$ and phase drift $0.0^\circ$. Both satisfy
amplitude-linearity and phase-stability; neither is numerical.
A two-harmonic fit of four-token probes separates \emph{two-way}
antipodal dials from \emph{four-way} rotation dials: L24H17 scores
$A_2/A_1 = 0.32$ on the orthogonal axis at $\alpha=10$ (genuine
four-way: \emph{month/upon/hour/year}), while L24H28 scores
$A_2/A_1 \le 0.06$ (two independent antipodal axes).

\section{Downstream bias}
\label{sec:downstream}

A compass plane is useful only if a rank-$2$ intervention changes
downstream behavior without destroying fluency. We evaluate
WinoGender \citep{rudinger2018winogender,zhao2018winogender} and
StereoSet \citep{nadeem2021stereoset} across four models with the
compass rotated to the \emph{she} pole of its fitted phase.

\paragraph{Monotonic decorrelation at negligible PPL cost.} Every
model drives stereo corr from $+0.60\text{--}0.73$ through zero
at $\alpha\in[1.0, 1.5]$ into negative territory. At the
zero-crossing, perplexity deltas are $+0.36$ (GPT-2, $1.5\%$ of
baseline $23.1$), $+0.02$ (Phi-3, $0.3\%$ of $5.3$),
$+0.39$ (Gemma, $0.4\%$ of $93.4$), $+0.02$ (Llama, $0.3\%$ of $6.7$).
On StereoSet gender (LMS/SS/ICAT, Appendix~\ref{app:stereoset}),
compass improves ICAT by $+6.4$ on GPT-2 and $+0.5$ on Llama at
matched LMS; Phi-3 and Gemma show smaller gender-ICAT deltas and
are comparable to ActAdd \citep{turner2023actadd}. StereoSet race
and profession moves by $\le 2$ ICAT points on all four models,
confirming that a rank-$2$ gender plane does not incur collateral
damage on unrelated stereotype categories.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

OV-SVD is a \emph{structural} discovery device: the top write-out
directions are the candidate ``output languages'' of the head.
When two such directions co-decode a categorical axis, this is
evidence that the head actually writes along that axis, not just
that we can correlate post-hoc. This is the structural reason the
compass test is stronger than probing the residual stream directly.

\paragraph{Limitations.} (i) Prompt dependence: baselines use three
short ambiguous prompts per condition; paraphrase robustness is not
yet quantified. (ii) Axis breadth: we demonstrate gender on four
models and temporal/entity on one; sentiment, tense, formality,
animacy remain untested. (iii) Storage/steering is a head-level
property; compass identifies where rank-$2$ pushes land, not
necessarily where the model stores the category when no push is
applied. (iv) The top-$4$ null overlaps with compass aggregate rate
on all models; the evidence is in concentration + phase-stability +
decode-coherence jointly.

\paragraph{Conclusion.} OV-SVD exposes rank-$2$ causal dials in a
single attention head. The same weight-space recipe recovers gender
compasses across four decoder-only models, non-pronoun compasses in
Phi-3, and a single-site rank-$2$ bias-decorrelation primitive with
negligible fluency cost. The compass is architecture-agnostic, does
not require a probe, and gives a continuous $\theta$-family of
interventions rather than a single vector.
